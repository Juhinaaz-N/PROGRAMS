STREAM-FLOW

Streamflow is the way in which water moves within a stream channel. Factors that affect streamflow includes The source of the water,The velocity (speed and direction) of the water & Any barriers to the water.Stream sources are rain, melting snow, or the ground below the stream. These sources can influence how the stream flows and the stream's velocity. This velocity will characterizes the streamflow.

LAMINAR-FLOW

Laminar flow definition is the straight, quiet flow of water without any visible physical barriers. It means that the water particles flow in parallel layers without physiological disruptions between them. The water particles stay within their layer as they move along the stream because there aren't any rocks or other physical barriers to change their direction. The water flows down the stream channel in a straight line, typically quiet with low velocity. In laminar flow, the stream moves in an orderly manner which is typically characteristic of the more basic type of streamflow.

TURBULENT-FLOW

Turbulent flow definition is the randomly directed, loud flow of water due to visible physical barriers, such as rocks. It means the water particles don't remain within parallel layers when flowing in a stream. In this case, the water particles collide with the barriers which force them to mix between the parallel layers. The water moves in random and erratic directions within the stream channel and is typically loud with high velocities. The louder sound is caused by the water's collision with barriers which moves the water around forcefully.In turbulent flow, the stream does not move in an orderly manner and is a more complex type of streamflow.
